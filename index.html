About Me

My name is Samira Vafay-Eslahi, and I will be joining the University of North Florida in Fall 2026 as an Assistant Professor in Electrical Engineering.

I received my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Texas A&M University in 2022.

After my Ph.D., I joined the Gordon Center for Medical Imaging at Harvard Medical School and Massachusetts General Hospital as a T32 postdoctoral research fellow, conducting research in medical image processing for two years (2022-2024).

Following my postdoctoral fellowship, in 2025, I joined Southern Illinois University Carbondale’s School of Electrical, Computer and Biomedical Engineering as an Assistant Professor of Practice in Electrical Engineering.

Research Focus

Medical imaging, signal processing, and hardware design

Research Interest

My research focuses on medical imaging at the intersection of engineering and radiology.

Instructor

ECE 578 - Digital Image Processing II, 2026

  • Advance image processing tools and algorithms including image reconstruction, segmentation, registration, compression, restoration, denoising, and machine learning.

ECE 558 - Digital Image Processing I, 2025

  • Introduces fundamental concepts and techniques for analyzing, enhancing, and extracting information from digital images using computational methods.
  • Covers topics such as filtering, segmentation, feature extraction, and image transformation. Includes hands-on projects utilizing tools like MATLAB and Python to reinforce theoretical concepts through practical application.

BME 536 - Biomedical Signal Analysis, 2025

  • Explored methods for analyzing and processing physiological signals using Fourier transforms, time-frequency analysis, feature extraction, and machine learning techniques.
  • Engaged students with real-world biomedical data, including Electrocardiogram (ECG) and EEG signals, to develop practical skills in signal interpretation and algorithm design for healthcare and research applications.

BME 533 - Speech Processing, 2025

  • Introduced core concepts in speech processing, including speech production, perception, auditory modeling, signal coding, and acoustic feature extraction.
  • Covered fundamental natural language processing (NLP) topics such as text classification, language modeling, and sentiment analysis.
  • Explored advanced machine learning approaches including neural networks, transformers, and large language models (LLMs), connecting signal-level processing with modern AI techniques.

ENGR 222 - Computational Methods for Engineers and Technologists, 2025

  • Introduced C++ programming with an emphasis on core concepts, language features, and applications in engineering problem-solving.
  • Covered development environment setup, control flow, functions, memory management, and data structures.
  • Explored object-oriented programming principles including classes, inheritance, polymorphism, and the C++ Standard Library.
  • Engaged students in hands-on exercises and projects to develop practical programming skills and apply computational methods to real-world engineering challenges.

Teaching Assistant

ECEN 248 - Digital Systems Design

About

I will be joining the University of North Florida, College of Computing, Engineering and Construction, in Fall 2026 as an Assistant Professor in Electrical Engineering. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Texas A&M University, Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering in 2022. After completing my Ph.D., I conducted a two-year (2022–2024) postdoctoral research fellowship at the Gordon Center for Medical Imaging, Harvard Medical School and Massachusetts General Hospital. In 2025, I joined Southern Illinois University Carbondale as an Assistant Professor of Practice, where I taught courses including Digital Image Processing, Biomedical Signal Analysis, and Computational Methods for Engineers and Technologists.

Research

My research focuses on advancing medical imaging at the intersection of electrical engineering, computer science, and radiology.

  • Signal and image processing in medical imaging
  • Medical image quantification
  • Medical imaging hardware design

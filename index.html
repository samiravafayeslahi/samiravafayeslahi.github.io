About

I will be joining the University of North Florida, College of Computing, Engineering and Construction, in Fall 2026 as an Assistant Professor in Electrical Engineering. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Texas A&M University, Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering in 2022. After completing my Ph.D., I conducted a two-year (2022–2024) postdoctoral research fellowship at the Gordon Center for Medical Imaging at Harvard Medical School and Massachusetts General Hospital. In 2025, I joined Southern Illinois University Carbondale as an Assistant Professor of Practice, where I taught courses including Digital Image Processing, Biomedical Signal Analysis, and Computational Methods for Engineers and Technologists.

Research Interest

My research focuses on advancing medical imaging at the intersection of electrical engineering, computer science, and radiology.

Research Projects:

  • Signal and Image Processing in Medical Imaging
    • Medical Image Reconstruction
    • Medical Image Quantification
    • Medical Image Denoising
  • Medical Imaging Hardware Design and Development

Courses Taught

Digital Image Processing II (ECE 578), SIUC, 2026

  • Advanced image processing tools and algorithms, including image reconstruction, segmentation, registration, compression, restoration, denoising, and machine learning.

Digital Image Processing I (ECE 558), SIUC, 2025

  • Introduces fundamental concepts and techniques for analyzing, enhancing, and extracting information from digital images using computational methods.
  • Covers topics such as filtering, segmentation, feature extraction, and image transformation.
  • Includes hands-on projects utilizing MATLAB and Python to reinforce theoretical concepts through practical application.

Biomedical Signal Analysis (BME 536), SIUC, 2025

  • Explores methods for analyzing and processing physiological signals using Fourier transforms, time-frequency analysis, feature extraction, and machine learning techniques.
  • Engages students with real-world biomedical data, including ECG and EEG signals, to develop practical skills in signal interpretation and algorithm design for healthcare and research applications.

Speech Processing (BME 533), SIUC, 2025

  • Introduces core concepts in speech processing, including speech production, perception, auditory modeling, signal coding, and acoustic feature extraction.
  • Covers natural language processing topics such as text classification, language modeling, and sentiment analysis.
  • Explores advanced machine learning approaches including neural networks, transformers, and large language models.

Computational Methods for Engineers and Technologists (ENGR 222), SIUC, 2025

  • Introduces C++ programming and engineering problem-solving.
  • Covers control flow, functions, memory management, and data structures.
  • Explores object-oriented programming, inheritance, polymorphism, and the C++ Standard Library.
  • Includes hands-on exercises and projects applying computational methods to real-world engineering challenges.
